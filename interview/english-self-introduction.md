# English Self-Introduction

This document is a practical interview script package for senior commercial roles.

## Main Version

Hello, and thank you for the opportunity to speak with you today.

My name is Alex Xu, and I have more than 20 years of experience in sales leadership, business development, and key account management across the electronics components, industrial automation, and optical communications sectors.

I built my career in leading Japanese companies including TDK, OMRON, and Fujikura. Over time, my scope expanded from managing regional accounts in East China to leading business across Greater China, with full responsibility for revenue growth, strategic accounts, team leadership, and market development.

In my most recent role at Fujikura, I led a 19-person sales organization across Shanghai, Beijing, and Shenzhen, with ownership of a CNY 400 million business. I managed relationships with major customers such as China Mobile, China Telecom, Huawei, and ZTE, while also supporting headquarters in Japan and Europe with market intelligence, strategic recommendations, and growth opportunities in areas such as 5G, FTTH, and data center infrastructure.

Earlier in my career, I also built new markets from zero. For example, at OMRON, I independently established the Central China business in Wuhan and delivered first-year revenue from a standing start. Across different roles, I have consistently combined frontline customer engagement, team management, and strategic commercial thinking.

What I believe differentiates me is that I am not only a sales leader, but also someone who can connect market insight, customer strategy, and execution. I am comfortable working with headquarters, distributors, key accounts, and internal teams, and I enjoy building business in complex and competitive markets.

I am now looking for a role where I can bring that combination of commercial leadership, business development, and market advisory experience to create meaningful growth.

## Sales Director Version

Hello, I’m Alex Xu.

I have over 20 years of experience in sales leadership within Japanese industrial and technology companies, mainly TDK, OMRON, and Fujikura. My background covers team leadership, P and L ownership, strategic account development, and regional growth across Greater China.

In my latest role at Fujikura, I led a 19-person sales team across multiple cities and managed an annual business of around CNY 400 million. My responsibilities included sales strategy, revenue forecasting, pipeline management, key customer relationships, and team performance. I also worked closely with headquarters on product strategy and market priorities.

What makes me effective as a Sales Director is that I focus on both results and structure. I can grow revenue, but I also build the team discipline, market focus, and execution rhythm needed to sustain growth over time.

That is why I believe I am a strong fit for a senior sales leadership role.

## BDM Version

Hello, I’m Alex Xu, and my core strength is building new business in technically complex markets.

For the past 20 years, I have worked in commercial roles across electronic components, industrial automation, and optical communications. A major part of my career has been identifying new market opportunities, opening new customer segments, and turning early signals into revenue.

At OMRON, I independently set up the Central China business and delivered revenue in the first year. At Fujikura, I opened new growth areas such as banking and government data center projects through carrier and OEM channels. I also identified 5G and data center opportunities early and translated those market trends into business plans and pipeline development.

I enjoy BDM work because it combines market sensing, customer discovery, internal alignment, and commercial execution. I am comfortable going from strategy discussion to field execution, especially when the market is changing fast and the business needs someone who can create momentum from scratch.

That combination is what I would like to bring to your business development role.

## KAM Version

Hello, I’m Alex Xu.

My background is in senior sales and key account management, with more than 20 years of experience handling major strategic customers in China for companies such as TDK, OMRON, and Fujikura.

Throughout my career, I have managed complex accounts across telecom, industrial, and electronics sectors. In Fujikura, I was responsible for strategic relationships with customers such as China Mobile, China Telecom, Huawei, and ZTE. Earlier at TDK and OMRON, I also managed large Japanese and multinational accounts in East and Central China.

My approach to key account management is not just relationship maintenance. I focus on understanding the customer’s business direction, mapping stakeholders, identifying long-term opportunities, and aligning internal resources to create strategic value. That is how I have been able to grow share, improve customer trust, and turn key accounts into long-term growth engines.

I believe that experience makes me well suited for a senior KAM role with high-value and high-complexity customers.

## Senior Consulting Version

Hello, I’m Alex Xu, and in addition to my commercial leadership background, I bring a strong market advisory perspective.

I spent over 20 years working in technology-driven industries including electronic components, industrial automation, and optical communications. Over time, my role evolved beyond sales management into a position where I was also advising headquarters on China market trends, customer behavior, competitive dynamics, and go-to-market priorities.

At Fujikura in particular, I became an internal subject matter expert on topics such as FTTH, 5G fronthaul and backhaul, data center interconnect, and the telecom ecosystem in China. I regularly translated market signals into structured recommendations for leadership teams in Japan and Europe, supporting product planning, commercial strategy, and investment judgment.

What I can bring to a senior consulting role is a combination of real operating experience and strategic analysis. I understand how companies make decisions, how customers buy, and how markets actually move, because I have worked at both the execution level and the strategic level.

That gives me a practical consulting style that is commercial, grounded, and action-oriented.

## Quick Use Notes

- Main version:
  Best for broad commercial leadership interviews.
  Target length: 90 to 120 seconds.

- Sales Director version:
  Emphasize team leadership, P and L ownership, forecasting, and performance management.

- BDM version:
  Emphasize 0-to-1 market entry, new verticals, opportunity discovery, and growth creation.

- KAM version:
  Emphasize strategic customer coverage, stakeholder management, account growth, and trust building.

- Senior Consulting version:
  Emphasize market insight, strategic recommendation, industry expertise, and HQ advisory experience.

- Delivery tips:
  Speak in a steady and executive tone.
  Pause briefly after each major section.
  Adjust the final paragraph based on the company, industry, and interviewer focus.
